ABOUT MALCOLM TODD

Malcolm Todd Hobert, known professionally as Malcolm Todd, is an American singer-songwriter, musician, and record producer born on September 15, 2003, in Los Angeles, California. He is recognized for his blend of genres, including indie pop, alternative R&B, R&B, and bedroom pop.

Growing up surrounded by music, he developed an early passion for songwriting and performance, shaping a style that blends pop, R&B, and alternative influences with a modern, youthful energy.

Todd began his career during the COVID-19 pandemic, writing and producing his first extended play, Demos Before Prom, which featured songs from his high school years. He initially gained attention by falsely claiming his songs were unreleased Steve Lacy tracks — a move that unexpectedly boosted his reach.

His commercial breakthrough came in 2023 when his singles “Art House” and “Roommates” went viral on TikTok, leading to his signing with Columbia Records.

Today, Malcolm continues expanding his reach through new music, live performances, and a growing global fanbase. Whether on stage or in the studio, he brings sincerity and creative vision that define his artistic identity.
